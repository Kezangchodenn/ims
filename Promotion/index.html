Promotion

Fifteen percent of 1,000 respondents to the Business Practices Survey said they were more likely to leave if their managers hired outsiders(1). But when it comes to hiring employees, internal hiring alone may not be enough to recruit and promote new employees for success and growth. Selection should be based on reliable promotional criteria.

A well-made promotion decision has the potential to boost morale and inspire trust in your staff. And more trust leads to reduced stress, less burnout, and higher retention rates (2). A poorly made decision, on the other hand, can lead to disengagement and resentment in your employees.

And in an era when remote employees are worried about being passed over for in-office employees (3) and companies are concerned about 'quiet-quitting,` you really shouldnt give your people reasons to distrust you.

This is why weve compiled a list of three promotion criteria for your organization.And while we arent laying down minimum requirements (after all, it’s not a box-checking exercise), we hope they’ll point you in the right direction.

      In most cases, We will base promotion eligibility on multiple factors.

    1. The common situation is to first create a hurdle. The hurdle is a prerequisite that has to be met before the employee can be considered for promotion. Typically, it's going to be the time or the tenure in the current job or in the current grade. For example, “you need to have a minimum of three years in your current position in order to be eligible or to make the list for consideration”.

    2. And then the second criteria comes in, that's performance. It's either coming from the most recent assessment or over a longer period of time. For example : “an employee has to be rated at the expected level of performance or higher in order to be eligible for promotion”, or if you're looking at a longer time period, “to be eligible to promotion, the employee didn't get a rating of “under expectations” in the past two years”.

      3. So those would be the three criteria and how they combined most often in a lot of organizations.
